Learn how to describe people, places, and things with adjectives, and build richer noun phrases.

1. Adjective Basics Level 1

  1. Position of Adjectives in a Sentence
  2. Adjective Order (advanced but practical) Size → Shape → Age → Color → Noun
  3. Fixed Collocations (Adjective + Preposition)
    • pleased with, afraid of, good at, interested in
    Learn the whole chunk — adjective + preposition — not the adjective alone.

2. Other Parts of Speech & Adjective Forms Level 1

  1. Nouns Used as Adjectives
    • fast food, rain coat → raincoat
    • soccer field (not grass of soccer field)
  2. Easily Confused: -ed / -ing Adjectives
    • bored (how you feel) vs boring (how something seems)

3. Articles & Quantifiers Level 1

  1. Article + Adjective + Noun
  2. Quantifiers & Countable / Uncountable Nouns

4. Possessives & Possessive Pronouns Level 1

Use possessives when talking about who owns something — in class, lost-and-found, or borrowing things.

  1. Possessive adjectives + noun my, your, his, her, its, our, their — must be followed by a noun
    • This is her new book. (not This book is her)
  2. Possessive pronouns (no noun after) mine, yours, his, hers, ours, theirs — stand alone; the noun is already known
    • Is this backpack yours? — No, it’s mine.
    • His coat is blue. Hers is red. (hers = her coat)
  3. Adjective vs pronoun — when to use which
    • + noun → possessive adjective: my phone, their classroom
    • noun already said / obvious → possessive pronoun: The phone is mine.
  4. ’s for people & things e.g. Tom’s desk, the school’s library — another way to show ownership

5. Comparatives & Superlatives Level 2

  1. tall → taller → the tallest
  2. Regular changes (big → bigger) and irregular forms (good → better → best)
  3. Patterns
    • A is taller than B.
    • He is the tallest in the class.

Learn how adverbs modify verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs, and how conjunctions connect words, phrases, and clauses.

1. Adjectives vs Adverbs Level 1

Essential side-by-side contrast — teach how adjectives and adverbs differ in form and function.

2. Adverb Position & Negation Level 1

  1. Position of Adverbs in a Sentence
  2. Negation and Adverb Position

3. Types of Adverbs Level 1

  1. Degree Adverbs very, too, quite, really
  2. Time, Place & Manner Adverbs (and prepositions)
    • Time: yesterday, last night, then, after …
    • Place: here, there, in the room
    • Manner: quickly, carefully, well
  3. Adverbs in Comparatives & Superlatives
    • He runs faster than me.
    • She works the hardest.

4. Sentence Connectors (Logical Adverbs / Conjunctions) Level 1

Link two sentences with logical connectors (e.g. however, therefore, because, although).

5. Fixed Phrases Used as Adverbs Level 1

Learn whole chunks — not single -ly words. They work like “how” or “when” in a sentence.

  • stay up late (not stay awake last night)
  • come up with, all of a sudden
Memorize the full phrase, not word by word.
